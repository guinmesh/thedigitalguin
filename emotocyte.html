Overview

Emotocyte (/əˈmōˈtəˌsaɪt/). A physical cell or artifact that captures and/or assists in the experience of a certain emotion.

A digital, product-focused continuation of Looking In: Emotocyte. The major question that this project explores is, "Can I create a digital tool for trauma-processing, healing, and sharing? If so, what would that look like? What could that look like?"

This project is, thus far, independently completed, and will be fleshed out in the Parsons MFA in Design and Technology program course, Major Studio 2 (Interaction Design), under the guidance of Jane Pirone.

Process

Mariel Soto Reyes looking into a physical emotocyte, peering through the peephole into the internal exhibit. The process of this project, though currently in progress, has also been documented consistently. Over the course of this UX project, I engage with the following steps:

  1. User research and literature review
  2. Prototyping
  3. (Coming Soon) User testing
  4. (Coming Soon) Refinement
1. User Research and Literature Review

This stage consisted of journey mapping, interviews, and informal focus groups. I wanted to continue my work from last semester, so I chose a population that would fit the particular questions I was interested in and that I would have access to for testing purposes.

Mapping the journey of one hypothetical user, Charlie (they/them). Charlie is dealing with PTSD, and would like to pursue holistic care of their condition.

The three main themes are still consistent with Looking In: (1) the inclusion of botanical organisms (e.g., condolence flowers at a funeral), (2) the almost compulsory need for personal expression, sometimes with words, sometimes without, and (3) the desire for community in a time of grief.

2. Prototyping

I began prototyping in a 5x5 exercise (5 prototypes in 5 days).

Coming from a fine arts background, I often start my prototyping process by sketching, recording sounds / music / videos, and engaging in conversations about the built environment with my partner, Michael Thut (he is currently studying architecture).
